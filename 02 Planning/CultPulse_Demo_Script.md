# Cult Pulse — 2-Minute Internal Hackathon Demo Script

## Core Positioning

**One-line hook:** Cult Pulse makes workout impact visible immediately.

**Problem statement:** Members do the work, but results take weeks to show up. That perception gap kills motivation and drives churn.

**What to prove in the demo:** This is not another stats dashboard. It is a motivation and retention layer that makes invisible progress feel visible after every session.

## Demo Setup

Show only these three core surfaces as the proof:
- `Pulse Map`
- `Training Load`
- `Curo insight panel`

Use these labels consistently during the demo:
- `Built live:` Goal setup, Pulse Map, decay simulation, Training Load, Curo insights
- `Mocked / future scope:` Goal progress view, progress photos, holistic plan expansion

## 2-Minute Script

### 0:00-0:20 — Open on the problem

**On screen:** Title screen or Pulse Map hero screen

**Say:**

“Cult Pulse solves a simple retention problem: members put in the effort today, but they do not see the result today. Visible body change, strength gains, even weight movement all take weeks, so effort feels disconnected from reward. Cult Pulse makes workout impact visible immediately.”

### 0:20-0:35 — Explain the product in one line

**On screen:** Quick transition to goal setup

**Say:**

“It is a progress perception engine built around three layers: a living body map, a personalized training load target, and Curo, which explains what each workout is actually doing for you.”

### 0:35-0:50 — Keep personalization brief

**On screen:** Goal setting flow

**Say:**

“We start with lightweight goal setup. In a few taps, the member tells us what they care about, like weight loss, strength, or general fitness. That goal gives us enough context to personalize what good looks like, without making onboarding heavy.”

### 0:50-1:20 — Hero moment: Pulse Map + decay

**On screen:** Pulse Map with recent activity, then trigger decay time-lapse

**Say:**

“This is the core experience: the Pulse Map. Instead of showing workouts as a list of completed sessions, we show what your recent training is doing to your body. Different formats light up different muscle groups, and intensity changes how strongly they glow.

The real hook is decay. If a member goes inactive, the body cools down over time. Cardio fades faster, strength fades slower. That creates a visceral sense of lost momentum, without guilt. You can immediately feel that progress is something you build and maintain.”

### 1:20-1:40 — Supporting layer: Training Load

**On screen:** Weekly Training Load view

**Say:**

“Then we answer the practical question: am I doing enough for my goal? Training Load turns format and intensity into a simple weekly score. So instead of just saying ‘you attended three classes,’ we can say ‘you are at 17 this week, and your target zone is 15 to 20.’ It gives members clarity, not just activity history.”

### 1:40-1:55 — Interpretation layer: Curo

**On screen:** Curo insight panel after an HRX or Yoga session

**Say:**

“Curo is the interpretation layer. We already know the class type, intensity, and workout history. Curo turns that into useful, science-backed feedback in a smart-buddy voice. Not generic motivation, but context like: this session hit quads, core, and shoulders; your body is in a repair cycle; one more workout this week gets you back into your target range.”

### 1:55-2:10 — Close on retention + future scope

**On screen:** Return to Pulse Map hero, then briefly flash mocked future-scope screens

**Say:**

“That is why we think this matters for retention. We are not trying to accelerate results. We are making existing progress emotionally tangible between sessions. What is built today is the Pulse Map, decay mechanic, Training Load, and Curo insights. Beyond that, we have mocked next steps like week-over-week goal progress and guided progress photos.”

## Short Closing Line

Use this as the final sentence if you want a cleaner finish:

“Cult Pulse turns effort into visible momentum, and that is what keeps members coming back.”

## Speaker Notes

- Keep the word `retention` in the first 20 seconds.
- Do not oversell AI. Mention AI once in the opener, then let the product proof carry the story.
- Spend the most time on `Pulse Map + decay`; that is the memorable moment.
- Treat Training Load as clarity, not complexity.
- Treat Curo as interpretation, not magic.
- When you show future-scope screens, explicitly say `mocked` or `future scope`.

## Rehearsal Checklist

- Total spoken time lands between `100 and 110 seconds`.
- First `30 seconds` clearly explain:
  - the problem
  - why it affects retention
  - why this is different from streaks and stats
- The `decay mechanic` is shown slowly enough to register, but not explained for too long.
- Each surface does one job:
  - Goal setup = personalization
  - Pulse Map = visible impact
  - Training Load = adequacy
  - Curo = interpretation
- Mocked screens are clearly separated from live functionality.
