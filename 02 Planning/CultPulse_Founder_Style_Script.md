# Cult Pulse — Spoken Founder-Style Demo Script

## Main Version

“The problem we wanted to solve is actually very simple. Members put in the effort today, but they do not feel the progress today. And in fitness, that gap is dangerous, because results show up weeks later, but motivation has to survive right now. That is where churn starts.

So we asked ourselves: what if we did not wait for the result to become visible? What if we could make the impact of every workout visible immediately?

That is what Cult Pulse is. It is an AI-powered progress perception engine that helps members see what their workouts are actually doing.

We start very simply. The member tells us what they care about, like weight loss, strength, or general fitness. We are not trying to create a long onboarding flow here. We just want enough context to personalize what good looks like for that person.

From there, the core experience is this Pulse Map. This is the hero. Instead of treating a completed class as a binary event, we show what that session actually did to the body. Different formats light up different muscle groups, and the intensity changes how strongly they glow.

But the most important part is this: the map also decays. If I stop showing up, the body cools down over time. Cardio fades faster, strength fades slower. So progress stops feeling abstract. It feels like momentum that I am either building or losing. And that is a much more powerful behavioral loop than just showing me a streak or a calorie number.

The second layer is Training Load. This answers a really practical question that most members have all the time, which is: am I actually doing enough? We take the classes they have done, map them to intensity, and show whether they are in the right zone for their goal. So instead of saying you did three sessions this week, we can say you are at 17, your target is 15 to 20, and you are right where you need to be.

The third layer is Curo. Curo is not here to be a generic AI coach. Curo is here to interpret. We already know the class type, the likely muscle groups involved, the recent workout history, and the goal context. So Curo can say something useful and grounded, like: this session hit your quads, core, and shoulders; your body is now in a repair cycle; one more session this week gets you back into your target range.

And that is really the point of the product. We are not claiming to accelerate body transformation. We are making existing progress emotionally tangible between sessions. That is what helps members stay engaged long enough to actually see results.

What is live in the demo today is the Pulse Map, the decay mechanic, the Training Load view, and Curo insights. We have also mocked where this can go next, like week-over-week goal progress and guided progress photos.

But even with what is built today, the story is already clear: if members can feel progress sooner, they are more likely to come back. And if they come back, retention improves.” 

## Shorter, More Punchy Version

“We think a big retention problem in fitness is that effort is immediate, but results are delayed. Members work out today, but they do not feel progress today. So motivation drops before results show up.

Cult Pulse solves that by making workout impact visible immediately.

The hero experience is the Pulse Map, a living body visualization that shows which parts of your body your recent training is working. Different classes light up different muscle groups, and when you stop showing up, that glow fades over time. So progress starts to feel like momentum you are building, not just history you are logging.

Then we layer on Training Load, which answers: am I doing enough for my goal? And finally Curo, which interprets the workout for you in a smart, science-backed way instead of giving generic motivation.

What we are really building here is not another fitness dashboard. It is a motivation and retention layer for Cult.fit. If members can see and feel progress between sessions, they are much more likely to stay engaged.” 

## Delivery Notes

- Start calmly, not hyped. The first sentence should sound like a real product insight.
- Pause briefly after `That is where churn starts` and after `This is the hero`.
- When showing decay, slow down and let the screen do some of the talking.
- Keep Curo credible. Say `interpret` more than `AI`.
- End firmly on retention impact, not feature count.
